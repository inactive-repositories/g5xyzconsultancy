FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

 

Project Overview

 

  1. What is the purpose of the project?

 

The purpose of the project is to analyze the temporal and spatial variations of bike usage patterns in Perth metropolitan areas. We aim to identify areas with high bike usage, determine peak hours, and overlay this information with private car usage and crash data to identify infrastructure improvement needs.

 

 

  1. Who is the client for this project?

 

The client for this project is WestCycle, the peak body for bike riding throughout Western Australia. They advocate for bike riders and strive to develop and promote positive change in the bike riding community.

 

 

  1. What are the deliverables of the project?

 

The deliverables of the project include:

    • A dashboard (preferably online) providing data visualizations of temporal variation in bike usage, bike usage patterns map, and an overlay map showing bike usage patterns along with private car usage and crash data.
    • Analysis findings report summarizing the insights gained from the project.
    • Recommendations for infrastructure improvements and future development.

 

  1. What tools will be used for data analysis and visualization?

 

We will be utilizing tools such as ArcGIS for data analysis and visualization. These tools offer robust capabilities for mapping, data manipulation, and creating interactive visualizations.

 

  1. How will the project impact transport infrastructure in Perth?

 

The project aims to provide valuable insights into bike usage patterns and identify areas where transport infrastructure improvements are needed. The findings and recommendations will assist local councils and policymakers in making informed decisions to enhance bike infrastructure, promote safer biking conditions, and encourage mode sharing between bikes and private cars.

 

  1. How will stakeholder engagement be ensured throughout the project?

 

Stakeholder engagement is crucial to the success of the project. We will collaborate closely with WestCycle, local councils, bike riders, and other relevant stakeholders. Their input and feedback will be sought during data collection, analysis, and the development of recommendations. Additionally, we will conduct consultations, workshops, and surveys to involve stakeholders in the decision-making process.

 

 

  1. Will the project consider sustainability aspects?

 

Yes, sustainability is one of the pillars of WestCycle's strategic plan, and it will be an integral part of the project. We will explore how promoting bike usage and improving bike infrastructure can contribute to sustainable transportation options, reduce carbon emissions, and enhance the overall livability of Perth.

 

  1. How will the project ensure data accuracy and privacy?

 

We will ensure data accuracy by employing rigorous data collection methods and utilizing reliable sources. Privacy concerns will be addressed by anonymizing and aggregating the data to maintain confidentiality and comply with privacy regulations.

 

  1. Can the project's findings be applied to other regions or cities?

 

While the focus of this project is on Perth metropolitan areas, the methodologies and insights gained can serve as a reference for other regions or cities interested in analysing and improving bike usage patterns. However, specific contextual factors should be considered when applying the findings to different locations.
