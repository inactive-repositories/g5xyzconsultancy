Project Methodology 

The aim of this document is to outline the methodology of our Capstone Project. As part of the project management process, the project management methodology is imperative for our team’s success. This document further assists us in helping our team plan, execute, and close the project successfully. 

Project Scope 

Goal – This Capstone Project incorporates the purpose to examine both temporal and spatial variations of bike usage patterns in the vicinity of Perth City. The project aims to identify areas within Perth City whereby bicycle paths are used and to determine the peak periods of bicycle usage. Additionally, this project hopes to overlay the bike usage map with data on private car usage and crashes to identify and visualise areas that require improvements in transport infrastructure for mode sharing. Moreover, if available, this project hopes to analyse speed data to identify areas with the potential need for infrastructure improvements. 

 

Objective – The objective of this dashboard is to:  

1.     Identify the usage of cycle routes within Perth City (10 KM Circular Radius). Allows for private users (cyclists) and government agencies to visually gauge the usage of routes for potential modifications/upgrades. (Primary Map – Base Layer) 

2.     Presenting peak usage of routes incorporating both vehicle roads and cycle routes. Promoting private users (cyclists) to identify routes with high private vehicle traffic to avoid potential traffic congestion and higher risks of vehicle and cyclist collisions/accidents. (Layer 1 – Private Vehicle Usage) 

3.     Illustrate zones that contain high-speed limits (vehicles). This allows this map to be used as a tool to identify potential areas where speed zones/limits and busy routes could be a deciding factor for cyclists before commuting. This will factor in the traffic of both cyclists and private vehicles. (Layer 2 – Speed Zones) 

4.     Highlight private car usage, bicycle traffic hotspots and crash sites using timestamps. This would allow the client WestCycle to propose newer architectures to ease traffic flow and reduce the potential of collisions using past data of bicycle collisions. (Layer 3 – Danger Zones and Crash Sites)
